The Physics of the Golf Swing

Understanding the collision dynamics, material deformation, and aerodynamic forces that dictate the flight of a golf ball.

1. The Impact (Collision Dynamics)

The collision between a golf club and ball lasts approximately 400 to 500 microseconds. During this brief window, peak forces can exceed 10,000 Newtons (over 2,000 lbs).

  • Coefficient of Restitution (COR): Measures the energy transfer efficiency. USGA limits COR to 0.830.
  • Smash Factor: Ball Speed divided by Club Speed. Ideal is ~1.50 for a driver.
  • Deformation: The ball flattens against the clubface, converting kinetic energy into elastic potential energy before springing back (restitution).

2. In-Flight Aerodynamics

Gravity ($F_g$)

Constant downward force ($mg$). Counteracted only by the lift generated by the ball's backspin.

Drag Force ($F_d$)

Air resistance opposing the motion. Dimples create a turbulent boundary layer, delaying flow separation and drastically reducing wake drag.

Magnus Effect (Lift - $F_l$)

Backspin causes higher air pressure under the ball and lower pressure above it, generating upward lift, keeping the ball in the air longer.
