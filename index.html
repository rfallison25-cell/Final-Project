Physics of the Golf Swing

From the atomic compression at impact to the aerodynamic forces shaping the flight path.

1. Energy Transfer & The Lattice

When the 7-Iron collides with the ball, massive kinetic energy is transferred in less than half a millisecond. In our 3D lab, the ball is made of thousands of particles representing polymer chains.

  • • Deformation: The impact side flattens entirely against the clubface.
  • • Acoustic Shockwave: Energy travels through the core as a wave, creating high structural stress (visible as red in the 3D heatmap).
  • • Restitution: The elastic materials rapidly expand back to a sphere, pushing off the clubface and launching the ball.

2. The Flight & Magnus Effect

Once airborne, the trajectory is determined entirely by Initial Velocity, Gravity, and Aerodynamic forces. The club's loft imparts rapid backspin to the ball.

  • • Drag ($F_D$): Air resistance pushes against the ball. Dimples help reduce the wake drag, keeping the ball flying faster.
  • • Magnus Lift ($F_L$): The backspin forces air over the top of the ball to move faster than air under the ball. According to Bernoulli's principle, this creates low pressure above and high pressure below, generating upward lift.
